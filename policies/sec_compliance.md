## 1. Overview

Master Fund LP, as an SEC-registered investment adviser, is required to comply with Rule 206(4)-7 under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940, which requires:

1. Written compliance policies and procedures
2. Annual review of compliance program adequacy
3. Designation of a Chief Compliance Officer
4. Maintenance of compliance records

This document outlines our procedures for ensuring ongoing compliance with these requirements.

- **After Significant Events**: Following compliance incidents

## 3. Annual Compliance Review

### 3.1 Review Scope

The annual review assesses:

1. **Policy Adequacy**: Are existing policies sufficient?
2. **Policy Effectiveness**: Were policies followed?
3. **Exception Analysis**: What breaches occurred?
4. **Regulatory Changes**: What new requirements apply?
5. **Industry Developments**: What best practices emerged?
6. **Organizational Changes**: Do changes affect compliance?

### 3.2 Review Process

```
Month 1: Data gathering and self-assessment
Month 2: Testing and validation
Month 3: Report preparation and recommendations
```

### 3.3 Documentation

The annual review produces:
- Written report to Board
- Findings and recommendations
- Action items with deadlines
- Sign-off from CCO

### 3.4 Board Presentation

Annual review findings are presented to the Board including:
- Compliance program assessment
- Material exceptions during year
- Regulatory examination results (if any)
- Recommended policy changes
- Resource requirements

## 4. Chief Compliance Officer

### 4.1 Designation

- **Current CCO**: [Name]
- **Appointed**: [Date]
- **Reports to**: Board of Directors (for compliance matters)

### 4.2 CCO Responsibilities

The CCO is responsible for:

| Area | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Policy | Drafting, updating, and maintaining compliance policies |
| Monitoring | Overseeing daily compliance monitoring |
| Testing | Coordinating periodic compliance testing |
| Training | Ensuring employee compliance training |
| Reporting | Preparing compliance reports for Board |
| Examinations | Managing regulatory examinations |
| Escalation | Escalating material issues to Board |

### 4.3 CCO Authority

The CCO has authority to:
- Access all books and records
- Interview any employee
- Engage outside counsel/consultants
- Report directly to Board without management filter
- Recommend policy changes
- Halt trading pending compliance review (in consultation with CIO)

### 4.4 CCO Independence

To ensure CCO independence:
- Compensation determined by Board, not management
- Annual private meeting with Board (without management)
- Protected from retaliation for good-faith compliance activities

Automated surveillance monitors for:
- Unusual trading patterns
- Front-running indicators
- Cherry-picking
- Window dressing
- Late trading
- Market manipulation

## 7. Training Program

### 7.1 Initial Training

New employees receive:
- Compliance manual review
- Code of Ethics acknowledgment
- Personal trading policy training
- Information security training
- AML training (as applicable)

### 7.2 Annual Training

All employees complete annual training on:
- Code of Ethics refresher
- Regulatory updates
- Cybersecurity awareness
- Compliance case studies

### 7.3 Specialized Training

Role-specific training for:
- Portfolio managers: Investment guidelines, best execution
- Traders: Market manipulation, information barriers
- Marketing: Advertising rules, performance presentation

## 8. Regulatory Examination Procedures

### 8.1 Examination Preparation

Ongoing examination readiness includes:
- Updated compliance manual
- Current Form ADV
- Organized recordkeeping
- Trained employees

### 8.2 During Examination

Procedures during an SEC examination:
1. CCO serves as primary contact
2. Document all requests and productions
3. Employees speak only with CCO present
4. Provide only requested documents
5. Meet deadlines for document production

### 8.3 Post-Examination

After examination:
- Review deficiency letter (if any)
- Prepare response within deadline
- Implement corrective actions
- Document remediation
- Update policies as needed
